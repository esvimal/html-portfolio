PATENT DETAILS

Transradial Prosthetic Lower Arm (2025)

The present design relates to a transradial prosthetic lower arm intended for individuals with below-elbow limb loss. The prosthesis is configured to replicate natural hand movements by enabling finger flexion and extension, thumb abduction and adduction, and wrist flexion and extension. The integration of these functions allows for enhanced biomechanical performance, improved user control, and increased functionality in daily activities. The design emphasises comfort, lifelike usability, and improved quality of life for amputees.

Humanoid Robotic Hand for Finger Flexion and Extension (2024)

The present invention pertains to a humanoid robotic hand designed to provide cost-effective prosthetic functionality. The hand is configured to perform finger flexion and extension in combination with thumb abduction and adduction to achieve improved grasp versatility. The design incorporates lightweight structural materials, servo-controlled actuation, and a simplified control interface. Through the application of bio-inspired mechanics, the device closely mimics natural hand motion, thereby enhancing dexterity, grip efficiency, and adaptability across a range of tasks. This invention addresses the gap between high-cost advanced prosthetics and limited-function alternatives, promoting inclusivity and independence for users.
